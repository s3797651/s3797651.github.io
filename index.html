Project

In my other life as a church musician/organist/choir director, there is a need for two (2) things:
  •  An online database of hymns, anthems and organ music for church services throughout the church calendar. The Royal School of Church music has published a  book called Sunday by Sunday, in 2008, which is a list of suggested hymns, psalms and anthems in church season order. The problem is that it and most other similar lists sort by church calendar days only and it's only suitable for the UK. It doesn't give the availability to sort by season, such as "Give me a list of all the hymns suitable for Lent", or the ability to sort by hymn or anthem or organ music, which then shows what seasons it's suitable for. James Laster has written two editions of Catalogue of Choral Music Arranged in Biblican Order, 1st edition in 1934 and 2nd edition in 1983, but these are very limited and, being in book form, cannot be added to online.
  • There is also a need for an online database of Anglican Psalm chants. There are books of psalm chants, such as The Parish Psalter, written by Sydney H. Nicholson in 1932 and the St Pauls Cathedral, London Book of Chants, complied in 1997 and people, such as myself, of ten write psalm chants for our own pleasure, which we get our choirs to perform,. However, there needs to be an online database written where people can upload chants to be made available for to download. These chants always take the same format: 1 octet, 2 octets, 1 octet and they are season independant or psalm independent. The only qualifications required would be whether the chant tune is a happy one, in which case it should not be used with a sad psalm, such as Psalm 22, for example.
The project will be useful, because western Christian churches around the world do not have a unified database for hymns, anthems or organ music throughout the year. In Australia we use a 3-year sequence of bible readings for Sunday services. So, it usually follows that there is an old testament reading, a  New Testament reading and a New Testament reading from one of the 4 gospels at every church service. Over the last 2000 years composers have written a lot of music for all the readings of interest throughout the bible. Given that there are 783,137 words, 23,145 verses, 929 chapters in 66 books in the bible, there is a lot of material to write music to and virtually every composer who ever lived has written at least some music relating to parts of the bible. So, there would be aa lot of interest in a proper unified database that encompassed as much of that as possible.

There are already two main databases which people use, presently: the Choral Public Domain Library (CPDL.org) sorts choral music by name and composer and, to a much lesser extent by church season for sacred music, but it's not consistent. Also, a lof of the CPDL library is simply links to other collections. The Petrucci Music Library (IMSLP.org) also has a broader range of music, not just choral music, but again, it's sorted by name and composer. Other non-English speaking countries should also be able to have music stored in the database, including countries that do not use musical notation as we know it in the west. For example, some of the pacific island nations have an entirely different system of notation, but because of the handed down learning of songs from one generation to the next, a lot of their sacred music is not notated down and runs the risk of being lost forever.

As each country has a slightly varying church calendar, complying loosely with the Revised Common Lectionary, the database would need to be able to be sorted as follows:
  • By indiviaul Sunday for each season of the church year for:
    • United States
    • United Kingdom
    • Australia
    • New Zealand
    • Canada
    • and other countries which vary from the countries listed above.
  • By season only - e.g. Lent>hymn/anthem/organ music
  • By Sunday of the year - e.g. Easter Day>hymn/anthem/organ music
  • By hymn/anthem/organ music - e.g. hymn/anthem/organ music>season>specific Sunday>biblical reference
  • By composer - e.g. list every written by the composer, David Cundy and what season>Sunday>biblical reading it references
The psalm chant database would be much simpler, because you would only need to sort by two criteria:
  • Happy/sad (major/minor key) psalm chant
  • Composer- i.e. list all the psalm chants written by David Cundy
The technologies required would be something like an Oracle SQL database running in the back end. It would have multiple tables - Season, Sunday, Hymn, Anthem, Organ Music, Country, Composer, Biblical Reference. The structure would need to be well thought out in order to cover the possible sort scenarios that would be required. The front end would be written in HTML and may need some Javascript or another language to connect to the SQL database. It would need to give the user the ability to select according to the lists above. It should also give the user the ability to upload a piece of music, or a suggestion, or errors/omissions. It would also need to be able to be translated into other languages.

If the project were successful, it would solve the current inability to search sacred music on a number of different criteria. It would become a worldwide resource of sacred music. It would become huge, like Wikipedia and give church musicians access to a humungous catalogue of sacred music for their use in churches throughout the world. It would contain music, not just from the western traditions but from other traditions, such as the pacific islands traditions mentioned above, Asian traditions and African traditions. It would be a huge database of notated music from around the world.
